To further illustrate how the proposed Chain-of-Responsibility (CoR) framework handles conflicting affective cues, we include a qualitative case study based on a short video sequence. This case study focuses on a scenario specifically chosen for its inherent semantic ambiguity, where there is a rapid transition from a neutral state to a high-intensity ``startle response'' (Surprise), to ultimately ending in a joy moment filled with happiness.

In this case study, first we extracted 3 representative key-frames using a sparse sampling strategy. This allowed us to capture the fundamental stages of the emotional transition from the video. This case is particularly challenging because it requires a clear understanding of the temporal aspect. We compared four reasoning strategies: Direct Prompting, Chain-of-Thought (CoT), CoT with Reflection, and our proposed CoR framework.

While the core logic of each strategy (Direct, CoT, CoT+Reflection, and CoR) was maintained to evaluate their respective decision-making architectures, the prompts were uniformly adapted to include temporal grounding. We provided explicit frame-level identifiers and transition cues to ensure that each method could account for the video's chronological flow. This ensures that the performance gains observed in the CoR framework are attributed to its modular conflict-resolution design rather than a simple advantage in temporal context awareness.
